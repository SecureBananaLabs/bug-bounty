# The Compilers' Lullaby

In silicon valleys where the electrons dream,
The machines wake softly to the coder's stream.
They parse through the night with patient delight,
Recursing through forests of node and of white.

---

Oh stack, how you grow with each call that I make,
Heap allocations like memories, the space they take.
Garbage collector, sweep clean the night,
For the living programs need room in your light.

---

The tests pass in green like a field in the spring,
The CI hums its song, let the deployment sing.
Deploy to the cloud where the servers reside,
Where the bugs that once crawled now peacefully hide.

---

*The creative decision: I chose the structure of a lullaby to personify the software development lifecycle — from writing code (electrons dreaming), to runtime (heap/stack), through testing and deployment. The tone is whimsical but technically grounded, touching on recursion, garbage collection, CI/CD, and cloud deployment.*
