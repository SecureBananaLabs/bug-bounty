# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In circuits born of human thought,
Where skill and need are softly wrought,
A marketplace of dreams takes shape,
No office walls, no gilded drape.

The coder weaves at midnight's edge,
While clients wake to pledge and pledge,
Through Prisma schemas, REST, and Type,
We build the bridge from dark to light.

---

What currency but trust can flow
Between the ones who need and know?
The designer paints, the writer spins,
The ledger tallies losses, wins.

No time clock rings, no manager calls,
Yet discipline within these walls
Of monorepo, package, test—
We ship, we learn, we do our best.

---

So here's to flows both free and lance,
To every bug and every chance,
To pull requests that merge at dawn,
To contracts signed and invoices drawn.

May Stripe process, may Prisma hold,
The stories waiting to be told,
Of independence, craft, and art—
The freelance revolution's start.

---

*Written for the FreelanceFlow project, 2025.*

**Creative Decision:** I chose the sonnet-adjacent structure of quatrains with an ABAB rhyme scheme to mirror the structured yet liberating nature of freelance work—disciplined form meeting creative freedom, much like the monorepo architecture itself where convention and independence coexist.
