# The Audit Lantern

In a room of quiet terminals,  
a lantern hums above the keys;  
it does not promise golden roads,  
it asks for proof in twos and threes.

A patch is only wind at first,  
until the tests begin to sing;  
then every diff becomes a bridge,  
and every check a little spring.

The ledger waits with patient light,  
for work that stands in open air;  
not louder claims, not painted smoke,  
but something useful, built with care.

So let the signal outlive haste,  
let review make the bright line true;  
small honest changes, cleanly shown,  
can turn a bounty into blue.
