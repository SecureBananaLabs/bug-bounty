# Latency Hymn

In racks of dusk where cooling rivers sing,
we cast our queries into threaded night;
each packet bears a fragile, glowing wing,
and seeks a distant port by shards of light.

The clocks negotiate in quartz and flame,
while kernels braid intent through iron rain;
a checksum whispers every secret name,
and loss returns as wisdom, not as pain.

So build with mercy in your measured art:
leave room for faults, for humans, and for doubt.
Great systems last when craft and care take part—
not speed alone, but what we won't leave out.
