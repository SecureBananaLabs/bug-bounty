# The Freelancer's Algorithm

*A poem in three movements — submitted for the SecureBananaLabs bounty project.*

---

## I. The Request

A POST arrives at midnight,
payload wrapped in JSON—
someone in a city I'll never visit
needs a thing I know how to build.

No handshake. No small talk.
Just a schema and a deadline
and the quiet understanding
that code is a language without accent.

---

## II. The Work

I pull the repo,
clone a stranger's intentions
into my machine.
The diff is small. The problem is not.

Three functions, two edge cases,
one test that keeps failing
until I see what the original author saw—
or didn't.

Green. Merge. Done.

No one will know my name
inside the binary.
The algorithm doesn't care
who solved it, only that it runs.

---

## III. The Ledger

Somewhere a balance updates.
A dispute resolves itself
in the language of wallets and timestamps.

The platform hums.
A freelancer in Karachi ships a feature.
A client in London marks it complete.
Trust propagates like a signal
across a graph neither of them can see.

This is the new labour:
invisible, distributed, permanent—
every commit a small proof
that someone, somewhere,
showed up.

---

*Creative decision: Free verse in three movements mirrors a three-stage API call — request, processing, response. The platform's own metaphors (POST, diff, merge, ledger) become the poem's imagery. Tone is documentary, not romantic — this work is real work.*
