# The Review Desk at First Light

Before the dashboards warm, the brief is set in rows,
each promise small enough to hold and test by name.
FreelanceFlow keeps the morning quiet: who owns the work,
what proof will travel with it, which change earns review.

A proposal becomes a branch, a branch becomes a trace,
not loud, not vague, but shaped for someone else to read.
Messages pin the hidden edges of the task in place,
so scope can stop drifting before the invoice learns to move.

In the review pane, evidence takes the patient route:
screenshots, notes, a passing check, a line that changed.
The client does not need a miracle from the queue,
only a clear result that matches the work they ordered.

When delivery lands, the system keeps the handoff clean:
status, receipt, dispute path, and history in one light.
Trust is not a slogan here; it is a saved decision,
visible enough that no one has to guess what happened.

So the marketplace opens with a practical kind of dawn,
where skill is measured, work is scoped, and records stay.
Each honest merge makes the next agreement easier,
and fair payment follows proof through the same open door.
