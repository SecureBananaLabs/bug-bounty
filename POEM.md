# The Review Ledger

Scope enters softly through the queue,
with fields for price, intent, and name;
a worker reads what must be true,
then turns the brief into a claim.

The proposal holds a measured line:
not more than needed, nothing thin.
It names the proof, the branch, the sign,
and where the reviewer should begin.

The build runs quiet, tests declare
which paths still hold and which ones break.
A comment leaves enough to share,
but not a secret in its wake.

When merge light reaches every row,
the record closes clean and plain:
the client sees the delivered flow,
the worker sees the earned refrain.

So trust is not a louder pitch,
or luck arriving after noon;
it is a well-kept handoff switch,
clicked on, reviewed, and paid in tune.
