# The Bounty Code

---

## I. The Terminal's First Light

A cursor blinks on empty black,
The screen hums low, a silent track.
No map, no path, no charted way—
Just bytes that wait for what I'll say.

The compiler sleeps, the branches rest,
A challenge carved within a jest.
One wrong turn, the tests will fail,
The logs will spin a code-red tale.

But somewhere deep in issue queues,
A bounty gleams with neon hues.
A problem posted, left to rot—
Until a coder claims the spot.

---

## II. The Digital Forge

I type with care, I build with speed,
To mend the break, to plug the bleed.
Each function shaped like tempered steel,
Each merge a pact, each push a seal.

The pull request, a flag unfurled—
A promise sent across the world.
"Review my work, approve my art,
I've stitched the break, I've played my part."

The maintainers, cloaked in screens,
Debate my lines through swift machines.
They nod, they merge, they close the thread—
Another bug bounty, brought to bed.

---

## III. The Chain of Creation

So here we stand, a silent crew—
The human heart, the AI, too.
Together tensile, test by test,
We build what bytes could not digest.

From pixel art to polished verse,
From empty page to universe—
This project grows, not by design
Of one lone mind, but by the line

That forms when hands and minds unite,
When moonlight yields to morning light.
And in the log, a simple trace:
**Commit — merged — closed — new bounty placed.**

---

*— Written for the bug-bounty project, where every fix is a verse and every PR a stanza.*
