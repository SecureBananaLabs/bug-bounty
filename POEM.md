# Ode to the Freelance Platform

*By XananasX7*

---

## I. The Request

A client types a dream at dawn,
a spec half-formed, a budget drawn—
"Build me a thing," the brief begins,
"and ship it fast before it grins."

The job record hits the API,
past middleware it dares to fly,
the rate limiter counts its toll,
the schema guards each field and role.

---

## II. The Proposal

A freelancer reads and feels the spark,
she bids before the listing's dark—
her coverLetter, crisp and tight,
her estimatedDuration: right.

The Zod schema holds the gate,
no missing field will infiltrate,
her token signed with sub and id,
the auth flow works as it should be.

---

## III. The Build

Commits roll in, the CI runs,
the benchmark suite fires its guns—
p50 latency, bright and low,
p99 stays within threshold.

The admin watches from the panel,
each action logged on every channel,
disputes resolved, jobs moderated,
trust scores rise, fraud is abated.

---

## IV. The Payment

The Stripe intent is finally cast,
the client secret— revealed at last—
no stub id, no Date.now() lie,
a real PaymentIntent wave goodbye.

The webhook fires, escrow releases,
the freelancer's account increases,
transaction clear, both parties smile,
the platform earned its worth, worth while.

---

## V. The Merge

Pull request opened, tests go green,
the CORS allows only the seen,
JWT secrets safely set,
no hardcoded default, no regret.

The bounty bot detects the claim,
the merge is clean, the reward came—
For every bug we fixed today,
the platform works a better way.

---

*This poem was written for the FreelanceFlow repository as part of the open-source contribution bounty program. All stanzas reference real code changes made to this codebase.*
