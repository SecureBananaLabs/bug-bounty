# The Weight of Zero-Trust

*A poem for SecureBanana — on the nature of building secure systems*

---

## I. The Gate

Every request arrives as a stranger.
No face is familiar here,
no handshake old enough to trust—
only the signature, only the proof.

We built the gate not out of fear,
but out of knowing:
the kindest door is the one
that asks your name every time.

---

## II. The Validator

Somewhere in the chain, a function waits—
patient as a stone,
checking each field against the shape
it was promised the world would hold.

A missing bracket.
A string where a number lived.
The validator catches it quietly,
the way a good floor catches a fall.

No alarm. No drama.
Just a 400 and a clear message:
*this is not what we expected,*
*try again, more carefully.*

---

## III. The Audit Log

Long after the feature ships
and the sprint closes
and the team moves on—

the log remains.

Every action timestamped,
every actor named,
every anomaly preserved
in the amber of append-only storage.

Security is not a moment.
It is the accumulation of moments,
the pattern that only becomes visible
when you keep everything.

---

## IV. The Zero-Day

It lives somewhere we have not looked yet.
It has always been there.
It is patient.

This is not cause for despair—
it is cause for the test suite,
for the fuzzer running overnight,
for the bounty that says:
*find it before they do,*
*and we will call you a friend.*

---

## V. The Patch

Four lines changed.
Forty characters removed.
One edge case, finally handled.

The PR is small.
The blast radius it prevented
is not.

This is the work:
unglamorous,
specific,
necessary—

the poem written in diffs
that no one reads
until everything depends on it.

---

*Written for the SecureBanana security community.*
*Theme: the philosophy of secure-by-default engineering.*
*Form: free verse in five movements — each representing a layer of the security stack.*
*Tone: meditative, technical, grounded in the actual craft of building.*
