# Digital Sentinel

In circuits deep where data streams,
A sentinel of silicon dreams,
It watches packets ebb and flow,
Through cryptic locks no thief may know.

The firewall stands—a wall of light,
Between the dark and morning bright,
Each byte inspected, each flag scanned,
By algorithms finely planned.

---

We built the vault with open hands,
Then sealed it shut with shifting sands—
For every key we dare to share,
Must vanish into fingerprinted air.

The hash, the salt, the proof of work,
Where brute-force meets its final smirk,
And zero-knowledge whispers truth
Without revealing singing proof.

---

But even sentinels can tire,
When social flames lick at the wire—
The weakest link was never code,
But human trust, too freely owed.

So harden shells and patch the seams,
Guard well the bridge between the dreams,
For in this garden, ripe and sweet,
The banana of security—never obsolete.

---

*Creative note: I chose an allegorical form—casting security infrastructure as a "sentinel" in a garden—because SecureBanana's identity blends seriousness (cybersecurity) with whimsy (the banana). The poem mirrors that duality: technical precision in language (hash, salt, zero-knowledge) paired with organic imagery (garden, sand, flame).*
