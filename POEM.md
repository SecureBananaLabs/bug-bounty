# The Weight of Silence

## I. Morning

The kettle sings its one-note song
while sunlight pools on kitchen tile.
I pour the water, wait too long—
the tea is bitter. That's my style.

I read the news on someone's screen,
a stranger's grief, a distant war.
I stir my cup. The spoon is clean.
I've done this maybe twice before.

## II. Afternoon

The office hums with borrowed air,
fluorescent veins that never sleep.
My coworker clips her fingernails,
a sound that burrows, cuts, and creeps.

I write an email, then delete
the honest draft. The polite one stays.
The cursor blinks its patient beat
like someone waiting in a maze.

## III. Evening

The grocery store at half past nine
is mostly empty, mostly calm.
A child rides the mechanical horse,
one coin's worth of rocking psalm.

I pick avocados, press the flesh,
test for readiness I can't define.
The cashier says *have a good night*
and means it for exactly one line.

## IV. Night

The apartment settles, beams contract,
a radiator ticks its Morse.
I scroll through photos from a trip
I took to outrun something worse.

The bed is made. I left it so.
This discipline of folded sheets.
I lie on top, still fully dressed,
and listen for the weight of streets.

## V. Almost

Sometimes I think about the word
*threshold*—how it holds both sides,
the doorframe and the open field,
the place where one world slips and slides.

I am not sad. I am not glad.
I am the space between the two,
a held breath in an empty room,
a window with a partial view.

---

*Creative decisions: I chose free verse with a quiet, observational tone—domestic scenes that accumulate into something larger. The title "The Weight of Silence" reflects how ordinary moments carry emotional density when examined closely.*
