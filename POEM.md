# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In digital halls where seekers roam,
Through lines of code and bits of chrome,
A marketplace of skill and time,
Where talent meets the client's rhyme.

The freelancer wakes at break of dawn,
With coffee strong and IDE drawn,
To craft a proposal, sharp and keen,
For jobs that wait upon the screen.

No office bounds, no clock to chime,
Just pure creation, yours and mine,
From landing page to dashboard deep,
Where dreams in JavaScript do sleep.

The client posts with hope and need,
A project waiting to be freed,
From abstract thought to working thing,
Through messages and notifications ring.

Stripe holds the coin, Prisma the shape,
Of users, jobs, and each landscape,
That forms the data, row by row,
Where marketplace and meaning grow.

So here's to flows that freelance run,
To code that's written, jobs well done,
To every commit, large and small—
May your next sprint be best of all.

---

*Creative decision: I chose a traditional ballad form with AABB rhyme scheme to create a playful contrast with the modern tech stack described in the README. The tone is celebratory and welcoming, reflecting the "good first issue" label and the collaborative spirit of open source—while weaving in specific technical references (Prisma, Stripe, JavaScript, commits, sprints) to ground the poem in the actual project context.*
