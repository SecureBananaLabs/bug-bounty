# The Coder's Lament

In the glow of midnight screens we sit,
Where semicolons mark the edge of wit,
Each function call a whispered prayer
That somewhere, somehow, logic is there.

We build our castles out of code,
On foundations others have bestowed,
A library here, a framework there,
Stacking abstractions into the air.

The bug appears at half past three,
A ghost in the machine, unseen, free,
It mocks our tests, defies our flow,
A single missing dot below.

But dawn arrives, the fix is found,
A race condition, tightly wound,
In async threads that twist and weave,
A lesson only pain can leave.

So here we stand, with coffee cold,
Another deploy, another bold
Attempt to tame the digital sea—
And whisper: "It works on my machine."
