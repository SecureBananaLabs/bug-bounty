# The Dispatch Board

A client pins a need to the morning queue,
not yet a promise, only scope made plain;
FreelanceFlow labels what the work must do,
and turns a loose request into a lane.

A builder reads the brief, then names the parts:
the endpoint, copy, schema, test, and screen.
Small commits leave a trail of careful starts,
so hidden risk has somewhere to be seen.

The runner taps each route and keeps the time,
while review compares the patch against the claim.
A failing edge is not a private crime;
it is a signal bright enough to name.

Then escrow holds the milestone at the gate,
not as a wall, but as a measured pause.
Proof crosses first, the payout follows straight,
and both sides know the reason and the cause.

By dusk the board is quieter than before:
one card is shipped, one invoice finds its mark.
Trust is not thunder at the marketplace door;
it is the logged handoff that survives the dark.
