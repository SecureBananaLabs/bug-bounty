# The Bug Hunter's Lament

In the glow of screens at midnight's hour,
I trace the threads of tangled code,
Where functions hide like frightened flowers
And errors bear a cryptic ode.

Each semicolon, a silent gate,
Each bracket, a forgotten door,
I chase the bugs that twist and bait
Through loops that spiral evermore.

The stack trace reads like ancient verse,
A prophecy of what went wrong,
And in this digital universe,
I search for where the bugs belong.

They say that bounty waits for those
Who dare to read the source with care,
To find the flaws that no one knows
And fix what others wouldn't dare.

So here I sit, with coffee cold,
My cursor blinking, bold and bright,
A hunter in the digital gold—
I'll find that bug before tonight.

---

*Written for the bug-bounty project — a tribute to every developer who's ever stared at a screen at 3 AM, chasing a missing comma.*
