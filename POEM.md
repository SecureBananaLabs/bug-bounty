# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

---

In circuits of trust where the gig workers roam,
Through digital marketplaces they make their home,
No cubicle walls, no time clock to bind,
Just skills on a screen, and a client to find.

The morning begins with a pull request made,
While somewhere a designer adjusts her shade,
The writer in Prague and the coder in Perth,
All trading their talents for freedom and worth.

---

Each stanza of code must be clean, must compile,
Like verses that sing with a rhythm and style,
The backend like meter, it holds up the weight,
While frontend like rhyme makes the whole thing feel great.

A schema, a schema! The database calls,
With Prisma migrations and relational halls,
The middleware watches for errors to catch,
Like a poet refining each line to a match.

---

But what of the coin? Stripe whispers low,
Of payments processed and of value to show,
No middleman taking his cut from the deal,
Just freelancer and client, both making it real.

So here's to the flow, to the gig and the grind,
To the marketplace built for the new state of mind,
Where talent finds harbor, where craft finds its pay,
 neigh Respondent, Inc. — just FreelanceFlow's way.

---

*Written for the FreelanceFlow monorepo,*
*where TypeScript dreams and transactions grow.*

---

**Creative Decision:** I chose a ballad form with AABB rhyme scheme to mirror the structured yet flexible architecture of the FreelanceFlow monorepo itself — the stanzas parallel the layered backend (middleware, controller, service, route), while the flowing rhythm reflects the "flow" in FreelanceFlow. The tone balances technical specificity (Prisma, Stripe, pull requests) with the romantic tradition of work poems, celebrating the modern gig/freelance economy that the platform enables.
