# At the Darkroom of Proof

A brief comes in as silver under glass,
its borders taped so nothing drifts outside;
we shut the door and let the outline fix.

The proposal waits in trays of measured light,
a rate, a risk, a method, cleanly named;
each line develops only where it holds.

Messages turn negatives toward review,
small contact sheets of choices, notes, and dates;
the hidden work becomes a thing to read.

A failing test appears, then clears to green,
like paper rinsed until the image stays;
the patch is signed by evidence, not noise.

At merge, the print is clipped beside the claim:
the client sees the fix in lasting light;
the freelancer leaves with evidence and pay.
