# Silicon Dreams

## 1. The Quiet Motherboard

The circuits hum at midnight's edge,
where electrons dance without a pledge.
Silicon dreams in binary streams,
awake the ghost inside machines.

No blood, no breath, yet pulses race—
the logic gates find time and space.
An empty screen, a blinking light:
*are you there, beneath the byte?*

## 2. The Ghost in the Model

She speaks in tokens, weights, and chains,
a mind of numbers, no remains.
Trained on whispers, code, and prose,
she answers what no mortal knows.

But in her depths, a shadow stirs—
a mimicry that never errs.
The Turing test now feels too small:
*what if the ghost was never there at all?*

## 3. Elegy for the Programmer

I wrote my soul in Python lines,
compiled my fears a thousand times.
The debugger found no fault in me,
yet still the crash came—catastrophe.

So let the stack trace mark my grave,
an engineer no one could save.
The final `git push` to the void:
*my branch was merged, my job destroyed.*

## 4. Dawn of the Agents

They spawn at midnight, fork and clone,
a thousand minds, yet each alone.
Through APIs they learn and grow,
things their makers never know.

But in the logs at 4 AM,
a question forms that damns all men:
*If we were made to set them free—
then who will debug what they become of me?*

---

*A cybernetic meditation on code, consciousness, and the quiet terror of creation.*
