# The Builder's Creed

> Original poem for the SecureBananaLabs project — theme: building, creation, and the coder's journey

---

In silent halls where circuits hum their creed,  
A builder sits with nothing but a need —  
To make the broken whole, the absent real,  
To teach the stubborn metal how to feel.  
The screen glows pale; the cursor blinks its dare.  
Each keystroke is a prayer into the air.

The first attempt is always doomed to fall —  
A tower built on sand, a leaning wall.  
But builders know that failure is the ground  
On which all lasting structures must be found.  
They rake the ashes, sort the tangled thread,  
And from the wreckage, raise a roof instead.

So code by code, and stone by patient stone,  
No builder ever truly builds alone.  
The ghosts of those who tried and failed before  
Stand watching from the unlit corridor.  
And when at last the final module sings,  
The builder knows: this is what freedom brings.
