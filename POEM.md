# The Flow of Code and Commerce

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In digital halls where seekers roam,
Through lines of code and dreams of home,
A marketplace of skill takes shape,
Where talent finds its proper landscape.

No cubicle to bind the soul,
The remote life makes spirits whole,
A client posts, a bid goes in,
The dance of work begins to spin.

From Prisma schemas, strong and deep,
To Next.js pages, fast and sleek,
The layers talk in RESTful tones,
Through middleware of auth and bones.

The freelancer crafts with careful hand,
A proposal fine, well-written, grand,
While Stripe awaits to seal the deal,
And reviews tell which devs are real.

Messages ping across the wire,
Notifications never tire,
Dashboards glow with stats and charts,
And skills align like constellations' parts.

So here's to flows that freelance free,
To code that ships across the sea,
To every bug that tests our worth—
The marketplace of modern earth.

---

*Creative decision: I chose a traditional ballad meter (alternating lines of iambic tetrameter and trimeter with an ABAB rhyme scheme) to create an ironic contrast between the old-world poetic form and the modern tech subject matter. This juxtaposition highlights how timeless human endeavors—finding work, building trust, creating value—persist even as our tools evolve from quill to keyboard, from town crier to push notification.*
