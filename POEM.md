# The Architect's Code

I dig through layers stacked in time,
Where silence holds each careful line—
A bridge between the known and new,
Where logic learns to walk in you.

The compiler's eye, cold and precise,
Demanding truth, ignoring price;
Yet in its rigid, binary heart
A spark of art refuses part.

For every function, every test,
Is just a hope we confessed:
That something built from thought alone
Could feel like something like a home.

---

Creative note: I chose a meditative, architectural theme in three quatrains with ABAB rhyme to mirror the act of building software — structured on the surface, human underneath. The poem moves from construction to execution to meaning, just as code moves from source to binary to impact.
