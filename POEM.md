# Weather Map for a Working Market

The brief arrives as pressure on the glass,
a small blue front above the jobs page line.
Someone names the work, the budget, the pass,
and blank fields turn to latitude and sign.

Proposals gather where the contours bend,
each one a plotted route through time and skill.
The client reads for signal, cost, and end;
the freelancer marks the risk the weather will.

In messages, the scattered readings clear:
screenshots, API notes, invoices, scope.
A test run makes the quiet figures near,
and each green check becomes a bar of hope.

Reviews move east like daylight over rain,
not thunder, just the proof that work held true.
The record keeps the loss outside the pane
and lets the next fair contract come in view.

When payout posts, the map is folded flat;
the storm has passed through names, receipts, and code.
FreelanceFlow keeps the forecast after that:
trust measured well enough to take the road.
