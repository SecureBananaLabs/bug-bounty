# Interface Windows

The first pane opens on a scoped request,
small enough to measure, clear enough to send.
A client names the outcome and the risk,
and work begins with edges it can defend.

The second pane keeps proposals in line:
rates, proof, dates, and questions left in view.
No promise floats outside the written frame;
each click records what both sides choose to do.

The third pane carries messages like tests,
short signals passing through a guarded queue.
When doubt appears, the thread preserves its shape:
what failed, what changed, what still remains true.

The fourth pane waits for review to finish,
with evidence beside the delivered file.
Approval is not noise or luck or speed;
it is a mark that held its form awhile.

The last pane closes on a payout trail,
receipt, profile, rating, finished scope.
FreelanceFlow turns scattered trust to records,
and leaves the next clean window room to open.
