# The Escrow Clock

At first there is a scope,
plain as a note pinned beside the workbench:
what must be made,
what must be shown,
what must be true before the door can open.

Then evidence begins its quiet round.
A branch names the promise,
a diff counts each changed line,
and tests stand near the window
checking the weather before release.

Review arrives with a careful hand,
not to slow the craft,
but to ask whether the shape can carry weight
when another person trusts it
with their time, their account, their next task.

When the merge lands, the clock answers.
The work becomes record,
the record becomes payment,
and the marketplace remembers
that clear proof is the shortest path between strangers.
