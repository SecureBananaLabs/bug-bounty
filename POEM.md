# A Contract in Four Lights

A brief comes in with its corners clean,
small facts aligned in a careful row;
the scope light wakes on the dashboard screen,
and shows which paths are safe to go.

The message thread keeps steady time,
not louder than the work can prove;
each estimate trims the hidden climb,
then leaves a note the next hands move.

Escrow waits with a patient key,
no fanfare, just a guarded rule;
when tests turn green and both sides see,
the locked door opens, calm and cool.

If doubt arrives in a dispute,
the evidence stays bright and still;
the record answers, line by line,
where memory bends and tempers spill.

Then payout lands without a drum,
a quiet mark that trust came through;
scope, review, repair, outcome:
four lights for work that people do.
