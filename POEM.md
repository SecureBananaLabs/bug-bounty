# Dispatch Window

At the edge of the board, a brief wakes up,
thin as a ticket, exact as a key.
Names, budgets, skills, and dates line up;
the work becomes small enough to see.

Zod holds the doorway with measured hands,
turning loose wishes into fields that fit.
Bad shapes fall out into quiet sand;
good shapes pass through, bit by bit.

Messages cross in a narrow pane,
not thunder, not theatre, just proof.
A question, an answer, a changed refrain,
each line another nail in the roof.

Review is a lamp on the final bend,
green when the tests and the story agree.
A patch is not done when the typing ends;
it is done when another pair can see.

Then payout waits with its numbered light,
no rumor, no handshake hidden from view.
Scope, evidence, merge, and receipt turn bright;
the ledger remembers what the work went through.
