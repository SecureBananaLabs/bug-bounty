# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In circuits born of human thought,
Where skill and need are softly wrought,
A marketplace of dreams takes shape,
No office walls, no gilded drape.

The coder weaves at midnight's edge,
While clients wake and scan the pledge,
Of proposals sent through wire and light,
Connecting dark to morning bright.

---

What value lives in lines of code?
In APIs that gently load,
The answer hums in every task:
Each connection made, no need to ask.

From Prisma schemas, tables rise,
To hold the hopes and the goodbyes,
Of projects launched and invoices paid,
In this digital arcade.

---

So here's to flows that freelance free,
To autonomy's decree,
Where every commit, each pull request,
Builds a world from east to west.

No single stack contains the soul,
Of those who code and make things whole,
But platforms bridge the distant hands,
That build tomorrow's unseen lands.

---

*Written for the FreelanceFlow monorepo — celebrating the architecture that connects independent creators with opportunity.*

**Creative Decision:** I chose a traditional ballad form with AABB rhyme scheme to create an intentional contrast with the modern tech stack described in the README. The poem's tone balances technical references (Prisma, APIs, commits, pull requests) with the human experience of freelancing, mirroring how the codebase itself bridges human creativity with structured systems. The three-stanza structure maps to the three-layer architecture of the project: frontend (connection), backend (infrastructure), and the human element (purpose).
