# Circuit Court of Small Promises

The brief arrives with margins full of static,
a little map of work that wants a name;
we pin each clause beneath a patient lantern
and let the scope declare what it became.

The messages take seats along the docket,
each question sworn before the build turns green;
no flourish wins the room without a witness,
no hidden patch survives the review screen.

The tests rise up like clerks with sharpened pencils,
they count the edge cases, line by line;
a failing trace is not a public shame here,
it is the bell that makes the contract shine.

Then payment waits behind a tempered window,
not as a rumor, not as sudden rain;
it moves when proof and merge agree together,
a measured door unfastened by the chain.

So every task becomes a small proceeding:
claim, evidence, decision, trusted release;
the platform keeps its quiet court in session,
and honest work walks out with signed peace.
