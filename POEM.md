# The Verification Room

The brief arrives with corners squared,
its questions bright, its risks declared;
a client marks the work in scope,
and gives the unknown room for hope.

A proposal leans across the wire,
not loud with promise, just precise desire;
it names the craft, the time, the cost,
and maps the path before trust is lost.

Escrow waits like steady light,
holding value out of sight;
not a wall, and not a chain,
but proof that effort is not vain.

Review walks in with patient hands,
checks the build, the notes, the plans;
what passes cleanly earns its name,
what breaks returns without the shame.

Then payout clears the final gate,
quiet, exact, and never late;
the record closes, work stands true,
and both sides leave with something new.
