# Freelance Flow

A marketplace of dreams, where pixels meet the pay,
The screen hums low at midnight, another freelance day.
Beneath the glow of dashboard charts and job alerts on queue,
The builders and the dreamers find the work they're meant to do.

A coder in Jakarta breaks a bug at half past three,
A designer in São Paulo sets another brand page free.
No borders in the browser, no clock to punch around,
Just lines of code, a promise made, a project that is found.

The search lights up with keywords, proposals drift like leaves,
A portfolio of passion is the only one who believes.
They chase the green "Submitted" with a breath they didn't know,
They'd held since writing "cover letter" an hour ago.

The client wakes in Portland to a stack of bids to screen,
Three stars, five stars, foreign names, a global, unseen scene.
They pick the one whose bio says "I build, I ship, I care,"
Click "Hire," and now a stranger is a partner somewhere.

The escrow hums, the milestone lands, a payment finds its way,
A review left on both their ends at the closing of the day.
And somewhere in between it all — the ping, the chat, the thread —
A living was made, a skill was shared, a story's been read.

So here's to every freelancer who ships before the dawn,
And every client trusting that the work will be withdrawn
From chaos into clarity, from spec to something real —
The marketplace of makers, where the only rule is: deal.
