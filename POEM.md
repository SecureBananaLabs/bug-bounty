# Reconciliation Lab

On the bench, a brief arrives with clean edges,
scope clipped tight like leads against a board;
the client names the fault, the freelancer measures
where trust went quiet in the record.

A proposal lights the rows in order:
price, deadline, proof, and what will not be done.
No fog around the work, no borrowed thunder,
just terms that hold their shape under the run.

In messages, the trace begins to settle;
screenshots, commits, and test notes find their lane.
Each small receipt becomes a calibrated needle,
showing who changed what, and why it changed.

The review does not need a louder witness.
It asks for evidence, then lets it speak:
a passing path, a corner case made visible,
a fix that leaves the contract still complete.

When payment clears, the ledger is not ceremony.
It is the final reading from the glass:
work scoped, work shown, work accepted cleanly,
and both sides certain what has come to pass.
