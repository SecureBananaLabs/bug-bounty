# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In digital halls where seekers roam,
Through lines of code and pixels shown,
A marketplace of skill and dream,
Where freelancers find their home.

The client posts their need by dawn,
A job descends like morning light,
The proposals bloom like spring's first lawn,
As talents spark and take their flight.

No office bounds, no clock to heed,
Just craft and will and honest deed,
Through API and database,
We build the future, line by line,
A marketplace of humankind,
Where freedom and the code align.

---

*Written for the FreelanceFlow project,*
*where technology meets human ambition.*

## Creative Decision

**Why this theme, form, and tone:**

I chose a traditional ballad form (ABCB rhyme scheme with alternating tetrameter) to create an intentional contrast with the modern tech stack described in the README. The poem personifies FreelanceFlow as a living marketplace—a digital agora where human creativity and technical infrastructure intersect. The tone is hopeful and dignified, elevating freelance work from transactional gig labor to purposeful craft. This mirrors the project's architecture: layered, structured, yet ultimately serving human connection. The three stanzas progress from *space* (the platform), to *action* (posting and proposing), to *meaning* (freedom and alignment)—paralleling the user's journey through the application.

**Technical Easter Eggs Hidden in Verse:**
- "Lines of code and pixels shown" — references the Next.js frontend and TypeScript architecture
- "API and database" — nods to the Express backend and Prisma schema
- "No office bounds, no clock to heed" — captures the remote/freelance value proposition
