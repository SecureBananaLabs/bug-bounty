# The Checkpoint Booth

At the first small window, a brief arrives
with rough edges and a necessary name.
The booth light turns from amber to clear,
and scope becomes a thing both sides can carry.

At the second, a builder lays out evidence:
screens, notes, commits, the quiet proof of work.
Nothing grand is asked of the machine,
only that it remembers who touched what.

At the third, review moves like a stamped card,
plain enough to audit in the morning.
Questions do not vanish into chat;
they stand in line until they are answered.

At the last, the gate opens without ceremony.
A client has less doubt than yesterday,
a freelancer leaves with earned momentum,
and the platform keeps the crossing honest.
