# The Receipt Room Keeps Time

At dawn the brief is folded like a map,
its margins bright with scope and steady names;
the client lights a task beneath the lamp,
and trust begins as something small and plain.

The builder checks the edge of every claim,
sets tests beside the work like measuring glass,
then leaves a trail of proof around the frame
so review can see exactly where it passed.

Inside the queue, the quiet ledgers breathe:
messages, commits, invoices, replies.
No promise moves until the records meet,
and every handoff learns to tell no lies.

When merge-time opens, green across the door,
the payout bell is less a shout than sign:
good work was named, examined, paid for,
and reputation keeps the clean design.

So let the market hum, precise and kind,
where guarded rails let careful people run;
the strongest software is the human kind
that leaves a receipt for what was done.
