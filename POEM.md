# Merge Window

The issue wakes before the dashboard does,
small as a checkbox, loud as a door.
A runner counts cold routes in milliseconds,
then asks the logs what they are hiding for.
Nothing is trusted just because it loads.
Proof is the lamp we carry through the floor.

In the admin room, the quiet tables breathe:
users, disputes, appeals, a late refund queue.
Every button wants a reason and a trace,
every status change is written down as true.
The cleanest system is not the one with no alarms.
It is the one that tells you what alarms went through.

The payment rail is only honest at the edge,
where test keys stop and real secrets stay unseen.
Amount, currency, metadata, intent:
small words that hold a marketplace between
the work someone finished after midnight
and the trust that lets the screen turn green.

So leave a benchmark under every route.
Leave a lantern where the pixel shadows meet.
Leave a poem in the root like a receipt
that says the work was not just fast, but neat.
A bounty is a promise with a pull request.
A merge is where the promise finds its feet.
