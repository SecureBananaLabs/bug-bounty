# The Merge

In the beginning was the commit,
a single line upon the branch—
a whisper typed in terminal light,
a keystroke launched without a plan.

The pull request opened like a door
to rooms we'd never seen before:
strangers reading what we wrote,
finding bugs we couldn't scope.

We staked our egos on the review,
accepted change requests with grace—
each suggestion was a gift,
each approval earned its place.

The tests turned green at half past three,
the pipeline sang its CI hymn.
We merged at dawn, and with that click,
the world was slightly changed again.

---

*Creative note: I chose a reflective, free-verse sonnet form to capture the quiet ritual of open-source collaboration—the vulnerability of submitting code, the humility of review, and the small but real satisfaction of merging something into the world.*
