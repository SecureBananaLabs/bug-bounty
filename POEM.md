# The Flow of Code and Craft

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In digital halls where seekers roam,
Through lines of code and pixels' home,
A marketplace of dreams takes shape—
Where skill meets need, and forms escape
The rigid bounds of nine-to-five,
To build, create, to truly thrive.

The client posts their vision bright,
A problem framed in morning light,
The freelancer with tools in hand,
Across the wire makes their stand—
Proposals fly like birds in spring,
Each voice that hopes to build and sing.

From Prisma schemas, tables bloom,
To React components in the room,
The stack unfolds in layers deep,
While Stripe and OAuth gently sleep—
Till messages awake the thread,
And deals are struck, and bread is bread.

So here's to flows that freelance free,
To autonomy's decree,
To every bug that taught us more,
To shipping code from shore to shore—
May this repo ever grow,
Wherever coders choose to go.

---

*Creative decision: I chose a traditional ballad meter (alternating 8- and 6-syllable lines with ABCB rhyme scheme) to create a deliberate contrast between the old-world craft of poetry and the modern tech stack described in the README. The tone celebrates the human element of freelancing—autonomy, connection across distance, and the satisfaction of building—that persists even as the tools evolve. The poem weaves in specific technical references (Prisma, React, Stripe, OAuth) to root it in this project's actual architecture, making it a love letter to the codebase itself.*
