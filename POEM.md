# Code and Soil

---

**I. The Seed**

A seed of code, a thought so small,
A whisper on a terminal wall.
It dreams of growing, branch by line,
A rhythm constant, half divine.
The keyboard hums a farmer's song,
Where logic's right and syntax's strong.
No water here, no sunlit ray—
Just keystrokes building night to day.

**II. The Field**

Outside my window, green fields spread,
Where farmer's hands break daily bread.
They scatter seed on willing ground,
And watch the miracle unbound.
A shoot, a leaf, a golden ear—
The patient yield of half a year.
No debugger finds the blight,
Just weather turning wrong to right.

**III. The Harvest**

At dusk I stand between the rows,
The code deployed, the data flows.
The farmer wipes a sweaty brow,
And wonders at the autumn's vow.
Two kinds of work, one patient art—
The steady beat of mind and heart.
In every line the land is sown,
In every field by field, commit by commit, grown.

---

*Inspired by the space where silicon meets soil — both makers of worlds from simple beginnings.*
