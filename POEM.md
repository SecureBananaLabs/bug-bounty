# The Proof Orchard

In the brief there is a seed,
small as a line item, plain as noon.
Someone names the work aloud,
and the empty field learns its shape.

A maker brings careful hands,
not thunder, not theater, just craft.
Commits arrive like labeled crates,
each one showing what it carries.

Reviewers walk the rows at dusk,
checking the roots, the tags, the yield.
If one branch bends the wrong way,
they tie it back before the storm.

Then the merge gate opens quietly,
and trust becomes something countable.
The invoice is not a promise anymore,
but fruit weighed fairly in the light.
