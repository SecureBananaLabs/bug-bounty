# The Methodical Hunt

*A poem in terza rima on the art of security research*

---

We trace the edges where the programs run,
in layers wrapped in trust we dare to peel,
a patient search beneath the morning sun.

Each function call conceals what none reveal,
we read the stack, we follow every thread,
and press against what logic ought to seal.

The parser chokes on bytes it cannot shed,
an integer that overflows its lane,
a ghost instruction waking from the dead.

We map the surface, sketch the attack chain,
a null deref, a race beside the door,
what once seemed solid crumbles in the rain.

No malice drives the hand that seeks the flaw,
but curiosity and craft and care,
to see the code more clearly than before.

We write the proof, we share it in the clear,
so those who build can harden what was bare,
and trust is stronger than it was last year.

The hunt is not for glory or for gain,
but for the quiet knowing that we made
the ground beneath our software more humane.

---

*Creative note: Terza rima\'s interlocking ABA BCB chain mirrors the iterative,
cross-referencing nature of vulnerability research — each finding links back to
the previous layer and opens the next, just as a chain of offsets and constraints
leads from surface to root cause.*
