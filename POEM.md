# The Contract Table

At the contract table, scope is set down first,
not as a promise shaped from air,
but as a measured line of work,
plain enough for both sides to repair.

A proposal arrives with careful edges,
hours, risks, and proof in view;
the client reads the weight of each sentence,
then signs the path the build will move through.

Tests become the quiet witnesses,
small green lamps along the way;
they do not cheer, they only answer
whether the work can stand in day.

Review records what changed hands:
diffs, notes, receipts, and names.
Trust is not a slogan here;
it is the sum of checked claims.

When payout crosses the final threshold,
no one has to guess what was done.
The brief, the proof, and the ledger agree:
fair work delivered, fair value won.
