# The Flow of Code and Coin

## A Poem for FreelanceFlow

In circuits of trust where the gig-workers roam,  
Where the client and coder both build a new home,  
No office to bind them, no boss at the door—  
Just pixels and passion and code to explore.  

The marketplace hums with a digital song,  
Proposals like fireflies, bright all night long,  
A Stripe in the distance, a message pings near,  
The freelancer wakes to a project so dear.  

From schema to frontend, the stack rises tall,  
TypeScript and Prisma connecting it all,  
The monorepo breathes as the branches align,  
In the flow of the freelance, the stars start to shine.  

So here's to the builders, the dreamers, the ones  
Who trade in their hours for digital suns,  
May your invoices clear and your clients be kind—  
In the flow of the code, may your purpose you find.  

---

*Written for SecureBananaLabs/bug-bounty — because every codebase deserves a little soul.*

---

## Creative Decision Note

**Why this poem?** I chose a traditional ballad form (ABCB rhyme scheme with alternating tetrameter/trimeter) to create a deliberate contrast with the modern tech stack described in the README. The theme weaves together FreelanceFlow's actual architecture (monorepo, TypeScript, Prisma, Stripe, messaging) with the human experience of gig work—building trust across distance, the excitement of landing a project, and the dignity of independent labor. The tone is earnest but lightly playful, honoring both the technical precision and the emotional vulnerability of putting your work out into the marketplace.

**Form:** Ballad meter (common meter) — 4-3-4-3 stresses, ABCACTB rhyme  
**Theme:** The human infrastructure behind digital infrastructure  
**Tone:** Earnest, slightly nostalgic, quietly celebratory
