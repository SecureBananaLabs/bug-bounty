# The Architect's Loom

*For the builders who ship in silence*

---

We spin our threads in terminal glow,
Each function call a shuttle thrown
Across the warp of what we know
And what the spec has never shown.

The database hums its quiet vow—
ACID promises kept in rows,
While somewhere past the load balancer's brow
A race condition silently grows.

---

We lint our thoughts before they compile,
Refactor doubt into clean abstraction,
Push to staging, pause a while,
Watch the pipeline's chain reaction.

Green. Green. Green. The tests all pass.
The linter sings no warning song.
We merge to main, pour one more glass,
And pray the edge cases play along.

---

The platform hums with contracts signed,
Freelancers and clients meet,
Escrow holds what both assigned,
And reviews make the feedback loop complete.

But beneath the dashboard's polished face,
Behind each chart and metric's gleam,
A human typed with tired grace
The code that powers someone else's dream.

---

So here's to the PRs reviewed at 2 a.m.,
To the hotfix branches born of dread,
To every `git commit --amend`
And the features shipped from a standing desk instead.

We build because the blank file calls,
Because the empty repo is a dare—
And the best architecture of all
Is the one we had the nerve to share.

---

*Theme: the unseen human labor behind polished platforms.*
*Form: four quatrains in loose iambic tetrameter, rhyming ABAB.*
