# The Freelancer’s Compile-Time Psalm

I. Mount
The monorepo wakes before us in `apps/web` and `apps/api`.  
TypeScript types keep the dark at bay; migrations hold the line.  
Every endpoint is a promise, every schema a covenant.  
We ship by contract, not by hope.

II. Route
A job is posted in plain text, then parsed by Zod.  
A proposal travels through middleware, auth, and rate limits —  
three guards that do not sleep.  
The client waits in the dashboard; the freelancer types in `/proposals/new`.  
Neither sees the queue that sorts them.

III. Render
In the browser, the UI rebuilds like a tide.  
Search narrows by skill, by budget, by trust.  
Pagination is a mercy; a full-table fetch is a sin.  
Loading, empty, and error are not bugs — they are manners.

IV. Trust
Payments do not leap; they stream through Stripe-shaped channels.  
Reviews age like milk; notifications fan out like confetti.  
Suspension is a verb, not a whisper:  
admin logs every toggle, every ban, every reversal.

V. Merge
The PR is the poem’s final stanza.  
Hash-tested, schema-bound, benchmarked once against the thresholds.  
What we delete comments out.  
What we ship, runs.
