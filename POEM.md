# The Queue Room

At the first desk, a brief becomes square enough to hold,
a title trimmed, a budget named, the rough edge made plain.
The room grows quieter when scope learns what to fold,
and every open question gets a place to remain.

At the second desk, the tests keep their small lamps lit,
checking the hinges before anyone moves the door.
They do not cheer; they only mark what does not fit,
then wait for cleaner hands to pass the work once more.

At the third desk, review arrives without a crown,
reading the diff like weather written over glass.
One comment asks the risky line to settle down,
one green check says the careful parts may pass.

At the fourth desk, the merge is not a trumpet call,
only a key turned softly in a shared machine.
The branch lets go, the mainline makes room for it all,
and what was private proof becomes publicly seen.

At the last desk, payout follows evidence, not noise,
a measured handoff after the work has held its shape.
FreelanceFlow hums where clients, builders, and quiet joys
turn scoped trust into something no one needs to chase.
