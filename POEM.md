# The Ledger After Rain

A report arrives with rain still on its shoes,
small lines of proof, a screen, a careful date.
We read the break, then read the path it used,
and turn the panic into something straight.

The test is not a bell, it is a lamp,
a patient light across the edge of doubt.
It shows the place where trust became too damp,
then shows the hands that wring the water out.

A patch is only promise until run,
until the logs agree and branches pass.
The quiet checkmark is not where work is done,
but where the next reviewer sees through glass.

So keep the ledger open, calm, and plain:
who found the flaw, who fixed it, what was learned.
Good systems do not swear they fear no rain,
they drain the roof and mark where storms returned.
