# The Relay Room

At intake, every brief arrives with edges,
a shape of work cut clear against the day.
The room grows quiet, counting what it pledges,
then pins each promise where the hands can stay.

A branch lights up with measured, local proof:
small tests, plain notes, a diff that names its trail.
No thunder is required beneath the roof,
only enough signal that the facts prevail.

Reviewers pass the package down the wire,
checking each seam where trust and change align.
The merge is not a trumpet or a fire,
but one green mark beside a finished line.

At settlement, the ledger does not sing;
it simply closes, balanced, clean, and known.
Good work becomes an ordinary thing:
a handoff kept, a seed of trust full-grown.
