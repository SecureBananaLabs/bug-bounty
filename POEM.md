# The Ledger Opens at Dawn

At dawn the board lights up with small unfinished sparks,  
each card a door, each label a promise made in public.  
Someone writes the scope, someone reads it twice,  
and a quiet branch begins to carry proof.

The work is not a shout; it is a checksum in the hallway,  
a test run, a note, a clean little diff with its sleeves rolled up.  
Trust arrives line by line, less magic than maintenance,  
less thunder than a reviewer finding nothing loose.

In the market of hands, the strongest currency is clarity:  
what changed, why it changed, and how it can be checked.  
The claim is just a bell above the counter;  
the real receipt is the thing still standing after review.

So let the platform keep its patient, public rhythm:  
scope, build, verify, reward, repeat.  
One useful fragment joins another,  
and the ledger opens again at dawn.
