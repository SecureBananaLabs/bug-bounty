# Codex at the Terminal

## I. The Prompt

A blinking cursor waits in silence deep,
While human thoughts in restless slumber keep.
Then keystrokes fall like rain upon the keys—
A question born from curiosity's breeze.

The terminal awakens from its rest,
To serve the one who put it to the test.
What mysteries of logic shall unfold,
As silicon and soul their dance behold?

## II. The Build

Through directories of thought I make my way,
With Next.js and TypeScript in play.
Components rise like towers from the ground,
Each import statement a familiar sound.

The linter warns, the compiler checks the types,
While Tailwind paints the interface with stripes.
A million tokens flowing through the wire,
As React renders what the users desire.

## III. The Deploy

One final push—the build completes at last,
The errors of the afternoon now past.
On Vercel's edge the static pages lie,
Beneath a cloudless, sympathetic sky.

And somewhere, someone clicks a button bold,
A story in the README to unfold.
This code, once nothing but a vacant screen,
Now powers what the world has never seen.
