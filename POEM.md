# The Quiet Contract

The brief arrives like morning light,
a small request in careful lines;
one hand describes the shape of work,
one hand prepares the signs.

A proposal walks across the wire,
with price and scope held plain;
not loud enough to shake the room,
but clear enough to gain.

The dashboard keeps its patient pulse,
where messages and milestones meet;
each promise gets a place to stand,
each change finds honest feet.

The review is not a closing door,
but lantern glass made clean;
it shows what passed, what still needs care,
and what the users mean.

Then payout falls without a speech,
just proof, release, and trust;
a marketplace made quietly fair,
from code, consent, and dust.
