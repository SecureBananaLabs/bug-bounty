# Small Contracts, Clear Weather

FreelanceFlow begins where fog would start,
with scope pinned small enough to see.
A client names the useful part;
a builder answers steadily.

No handshake drifts beyond the rail,
no promise hides outside the page.
The brief becomes a weather vane,
turning proof toward review's stage.

Screenshots, commits, and notes arrive,
plain signals on a working chart.
They show the task is still alive
and keep the finish close to start.

When review turns green, the ledger clears,
not with thunder, but with light.
Escrow lets go of held-up years
and pays the work that shipped tonight.

So small contracts learn to shine
through evidence, trust, and timely flow.
Each finished ticket draws a line
where future clients know to go.
