# Technical Poem

## Silicon Dreams

Binary rivers flow through copper veins,
Where silicon dreams awaken in the dark.
Circuits pulse with lightning-fast refrains,
Each transistor leaves its tiny mark.

Through gates of logic, signals dance and weave,
A symphony of zeros and of ones.
In motherboards the digital spirits grieve,
When processing a task is never done.

The kernel sleeps, then wakes to serve a call,
A context switch, an interrupt, a sigh.
Through virtual memory pages, they fall,
Then rise again beneath a watchful eye.

In data centers humming through the night,
Where cooling fans compose their constant song,
The servers work with all their might,
Correcting every bit that goes along wrong.

So pause and think, when next you press a key,
The marvel that computes what you impart.
A revolution no one thought would be,
A universe of thought in every part.
